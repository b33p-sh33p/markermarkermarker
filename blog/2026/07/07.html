My attempt at restoring a $20 MacBook (Part 1)

7/7/26

A few weeks ago, I came across a mid 2012 unibody Macbook Pro, the same model that I grew up using, for only $20. Granted, on the listing it said that it didn't work, as there was no hard drive or RAM inside. Luckily, I still have access to all of the parts from my old MBP, even though it also doesn't work anymore. I figured that I could replace the non-working or missing parts from the eBay MBP with the still-working parts on my old MBP to get the machine working.

It arrived today. The first thing I did when I got it out of the packaging was open it up and put in a SATA SSD that I had laying around, as well as the 4GB of RAM that my old MBP originally came with. A lot of the screws on the bottom cover were missing, with only 4 left on the corners. So for now, I decided to put tape over the empty screw holes, so that dust wouldn't get into the internals--one of the issues I ran into with my old MBP was that the rubber feet came off, letting dust and other gunk in that would later damage the SATA cable.

After closing it back up, I tried to charge the laptop. The only charger I had for it was a very janky and old one with a lot of exposed wire, so I covered the exposed parts with tape and tried to plug the laptop in. It already had an aftermarket battery, which I was a little glad about because the only other battery I had (the original one that came with my MBP) was probably in much worse shape. However, the listing said that the laptop wouldn't charge at all - the light wouldn't even come on, so I figured there was a problem with the charging port. I followed this guide to replacing the charging port, using the one from my old machine, to no avail. It still wouldn't charge.

This led me to believe that maybe there was an issue with my old charger. It's a wonder it didn't explode or catch fire lmao. I guess I should've made sure I had a working charger for this device before I ordered it or while I was waiting for it to arrive. But I found an old MagSafe 1 charger in much better condition that'll arrive in a few days, so I'll update when it arrives.
